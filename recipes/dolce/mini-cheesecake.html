Mini Cheesecake

Mini desserts sometimes get a bad rap. They can be seen as gimmicky or as “lesser” than the full-sized originals they replicate. Not so with these mini cheesecakes! They’ll win over anyone on the wrong side of the mini dessert debate.
There’s no compromising the smooth, creamy texture or just-sweet-enough, tangy flavor of the original, and these can be dressed up or down just like any other plain cheesecake. Unlike a hefty whole cheesecake, mini cheesecakes are even easier to make (no water bath needed!), store, travel with, and share.

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 ounces (100g) graham crackers (about 6 whole crackers)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons (20g) unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 10 1/2 ounces (300g) cream cheese, room temperature
  • 1/2 cup (100g) sugar
  • 1 tablespoon flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 3 1/2 ounces (100g) sour cream (about heaping 1/3 cup), room temperature
  • Fresh berries (optional)
  • Raspberry sauce or jam (optional)
  • Whipped cream (optional)

Directions

  1. Step 1

    Preheat the oven to 350°F.

  2. Step 2

    Pulse the graham crackers in a food processor until finely ground. Add the sugar, salt, and melted
    butter and pulse together just until some clumps form, approximately 20 seconds.

  3. Step 3

    Add muffin liners to a standard muffin pan. Distribute the crust evenly among the 12 liners using
    a tablespoon.

  4. Step 4

    Add the cream cheese to the bowl of an electric mixer fitted with the paddle attachment. Mix just until
    smooth, about 30 seconds. Add the sugar and flour and beat on low speed until integrated and smooth,
    about 1 minute.

  5. Step 5

    Make sure the crust and baking tin is completely room temperature (if it’s not, wait until it is).
    Distribute the filling evenly into each muffin liner using a quarter-cup measure. Gently tap the muffin
    tin on the counter to release any bubbles in the cheesecake filling.

  6. Step 6

    Remove the tin from the oven and let cool to room temperature on a wire rack. Once cooled to room
    temperature, pull the mini cheesecakes out of the muffin tin using the liners.

  7. Step 7

    To serve, remove the liners, if desired, and transfer the mini cheesecakes to a plate. Serve as is
    or with a topping of your choice.

Hints

Use room temperature ingredients.
Use block cream cheese.
Don’t skip the flour.
Don’t overmix the filling.
Liners for the pan are non-negotiable.
Let them cool slowly.
